# Knowledge Engine Architecture

---

# Knowledge Philosophy

Organizations do not create value by storing documents.

They create value by understanding what those documents mean.

Documents contain information.

Information becomes Knowledge only after organizational interpretation.

Knowledge becomes Intelligence only after organizational validation.

Intelligence supports Decisions.

The Knowledge Engine exists to manage this complete transformation.

Its responsibility is not producing Artificial Intelligence.

Its responsibility is producing trustworthy organizational Knowledge.

Knowledge is never extracted automatically.

Knowledge is constructed.

Knowledge is validated.

Knowledge evolves.

Knowledge is governed.

Knowledge preserves organizational understanding beyond individual documents.

Without a Knowledge Engine, organizations accumulate files.

With a Knowledge Engine, organizations accumulate organizational intelligence.

Knowledge must always answer four questions:

- What is known?
- Why is it known?
- Who validated it?
- Is it still true?

Knowledge is not permanent.

Knowledge evolves as organizations evolve.

Every change must remain explainable.

Every promotion must remain governed.

Every relationship must remain traceable.

The Knowledge Engine is the bridge between organizational information and organizational intelligence.

---

# Knowledge Principles

The following principles govern every Knowledge capability implemented inside ENQI.

Knowledge is not treated as static data.

Knowledge is treated as a governed organizational asset.

---

## Principle 1 – Knowledge Must Be Constructed

Knowledge does not emerge directly from documents.

Documents provide information.

Information becomes Knowledge only after organizational interpretation.

Artificial Intelligence may assist this process.

It never replaces organizational validation.

---

## Principle 2 – Knowledge Requires Validation

Every Knowledge Object should have a clear validation status.

Possible states include:

- Draft
- Proposed
- Validated
- Superseded
- Archived

Unvalidated Knowledge must never become organizational truth automatically.

---

## Principle 3 – Knowledge Evolves

Knowledge changes over time.

Organizations evolve.

Processes evolve.

Policies evolve.

Business understanding evolves.

The Knowledge Engine must preserve this evolution without losing historical context.

---

## Principle 4 – Knowledge Preserves Traceability

Every Knowledge Object should remain traceable to its origin.

Traceability includes:

- Source Documents
- Supporting Evidence
- Validation History
- Responsible People
- Business Rules
- Related Decisions

Knowledge without provenance cannot be trusted.

---

## Principle 5 – Knowledge Supports Decisions

Knowledge is not an end.

Its purpose is enabling better organizational decisions.

Every important Knowledge Object should contribute to one or more business capabilities.

Knowledge disconnected from organizational value becomes maintenance cost.

---

## Principle 6 – Knowledge Requires Relationships

Isolated facts have limited value.

Knowledge gains meaning through relationships.

Relationships may connect:

- Documents
- Knowledge Objects
- Processes
- Policies
- Departments
- Projects
- Decisions
- People
- Business Concepts

Understanding emerges from connected knowledge.

---

## Principle 7 – Knowledge Must Be Governed

Knowledge ownership belongs to the organization.

The Knowledge Engine enforces:

- Validation
- Versioning
- Approval
- Permissions
- Lifecycle
- Audit

Governance determines whether Knowledge may be trusted.

---

## Principle 8 – Knowledge Must Remain Explainable

Every important organizational conclusion should be explainable.

The Knowledge Engine should always preserve:

- Origin
- Supporting evidence
- Validation path
- Relationships
- Confidence
- Responsible authorities

Explainability is mandatory for trustworthy organizational knowledge.

---

## Principle 9 – Knowledge Outlives Documents

Documents may be archived.

Documents may be deleted.

Documents may become obsolete.

Organizational understanding should survive these events whenever governance allows.

Knowledge preserves business understanding beyond individual files.

---

## Principle 10 – Knowledge Enables Organizational Intelligence

Knowledge is not the final objective.

Its purpose is enabling:

- Better Search
- Better Artificial Intelligence
- Better Workflows
- Better Decisions
- Better Governance

The Knowledge Engine transforms organizational information into organizational intelligence.

These principles govern every Knowledge capability implemented inside ENQI.

# Knowledge Reasoning

Knowledge Reasoning is the capability of traversing the Knowledge Graph to discover governed organizational understanding.

Reasoning never invents knowledge.

It discovers governed paths connecting validated Knowledge Objects.

---

## Reasoning Philosophy

Reasoning does not create facts.

Reasoning connects facts.

Reasoning explains facts.

Reasoning measures confidence.

Reasoning exposes uncertainty.

Every reasoning path must remain explainable.

---

## Reasoning Inputs

Reasoning may start from:

- Knowledge Object
- Entity
- Relationship
- Business Rule
- Search Result
- Organizational Question
- Workflow Event
- AI Request

Everything begins from validated organizational knowledge.

---

## Reasoning Pipeline

Conceptually:

```text
Starting Object
      │
      ▼
Relationship Traversal
      │
      ▼
Evidence Collection
      │
      ▼
Business Rule Evaluation
      │
      ▼
Confidence Calculation
      │
      ▼
Governance Validation
      │
      ▼
Organizational Understanding
```

Every transition remains explainable.

---

## Traversal Principles

Traversal must always respect:

- Organization
- Workspace
- Permissions
- Governance
- Lifecycle
- Validation
- Confidence
- Evidence quality

Traversal never bypasses organizational boundaries.

---

## Relationship Expansion

During reasoning the engine may explore:

- Parent
- Child
- Dependency
- Sequence
- Approval
- Ownership
- Temporal
- Business
- Semantic

Every relationship type has explicit business meaning.

---

## Confidence Propagation

Confidence decreases when:

- Evidence weakens
- Relationships become indirect
- Validation becomes outdated
- Business rules conflict
- Governance uncertainty increases

Confidence never increases automatically.

---

## Conflict Resolution

Reasoning may detect:

- Conflicting evidence
- Multiple valid interpretations
- Missing evidence
- Circular relationships
- Governance conflicts

Conflicts are exposed.

They are never hidden.

---

## Explainability

Every reasoning result must expose:

- Starting point
- Traversed objects
- Traversed relationships
- Supporting evidence
- Applied business rules
- Confidence
- Governance decisions

Nothing remains implicit.

---

## Final Knowledge Reasoning Principle

Knowledge Reasoning transforms connected organizational knowledge into governed organizational understanding without generating unsupported conclusions.
