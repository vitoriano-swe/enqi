# ENQI - Knowledge Model

## Status

Draft v0.1

---

# Purpose

This document defines the Knowledge model of ENQI.

Rather than describing implementation details, it defines the business meaning of organizational knowledge, its lifecycle, responsibilities, governance and relationship with every other domain component.

Knowledge is the primary asset produced by ENQI.

Documents are the input.

Knowledge is the product.

Intelligence is the outcome.

This document serves as the authoritative reference for Knowledge behavior across the entire platform.

---

# Knowledge Philosophy

Organizations do not create value simply by storing documents.

They create value by understanding what those documents collectively mean.

Knowledge is not extracted from a single document.

Knowledge emerges from the relationships established between multiple sources of organizational information.

Every document contributes information.

Every piece of information contributes knowledge.

Every knowledge object contributes intelligence.

Every intelligence object supports better organizational decisions.

```text
Documents
      │
      ▼
Information
      │
      ▼
Knowledge
      │
      ▼
Intelligence
      │
      ▼
Business Decisions
```

Knowledge is never static.

It continuously evolves as organizations generate new documents, new relationships and new business context.

This continuous evolution represents the central business philosophy of ENQI.

---

# What is Knowledge?

Within ENQI, Knowledge is a structured representation of organizational understanding.

Knowledge is created when information extracted from one or more documents becomes connected through business context.

Unlike raw information, Knowledge possesses meaning.

Knowledge answers questions.

Knowledge identifies relationships.

Knowledge supports reasoning.

Knowledge enables decision making.

Knowledge may represent:

- Business entities
- Customers
- Suppliers
- Contracts
- Products
- Services
- Projects
- Assets
- Financial events
- Risks
- Policies
- Procedures
- Regulatory obligations
- Organizational processes

Knowledge exists independently from the documents that originally generated it.

Documents may change.

Knowledge continuously evolves.

The value of ENQI comes from maintaining this organizational understanding over time.

---

# What is NOT Knowledge?

Knowledge is not simply stored information.

Knowledge is not a PDF.

Knowledge is not a document.

Knowledge is not extracted text.

Knowledge is not OCR output.

Knowledge is not metadata.

Knowledge is not an embedding.

Knowledge is not a vector.

Knowledge is not a database record.

Knowledge is not an AI summary.

Knowledge is not a Large Language Model response.

Knowledge is not generated by Artificial Intelligence alone.

Artificial Intelligence may help discover knowledge.

It does not create organizational knowledge by itself.

Knowledge only exists when organizational information becomes connected through business context.

Without context, there is only information.

Without relationships, there is only isolated data.

Without business meaning, there is no knowledge.

Knowledge is a business asset.

Not a technical artifact.

# Knowledge Relationships

Knowledge does not exist as isolated objects.

Every Knowledge Object exists because it is connected to other Knowledge Objects.

The value of organizational knowledge comes from these relationships.

Possible relationships include:

- Customer owns Contract
- Contract generates Invoice
- Invoice belongs to Project
- Project involves Supplier
- Supplier delivers Product
- Product belongs to Service
- Service supports Customer
- Customer creates Support Case
- Support Case generates Risk
- Risk affects Project
- Project requires Approval
- Approval produces Decision
- Decision creates new Documents
- Documents generate new Knowledge

```text
Customer
    │
    ├──────── Contract
    │              │
    │              ├──────── Invoice
    │              │
    │              └──────── Payment
    │
    ├──────── Project
    │              │
    │              ├──────── Supplier
    │              ├──────── Product
    │              ├──────── Risk
    │              └──────── Decision
    │
    └──────── Support Case
                   │
                   └──────── Resolution
```

Relationships are first-class business objects.

They may contain:

- Confidence
- Source
- Creation date
- Validation status
- Business owner
- AI confidence
- Human validation
- Organizational context

Knowledge grows through relationships.

Adding relationships is often more valuable than adding isolated Knowledge Objects.

The organizational graph continuously evolves as the company produces new information.

# Knowledge Graph

The ENQI Knowledge Graph represents the connected organizational understanding maintained by the platform.

It is composed of Knowledge Objects and the business relationships established between them.

```text
Knowledge Graph

├── Customers
├── Suppliers
├── Contracts
├── Invoices
├── Products
├── Services
├── Projects
├── Risks
├── Policies
├── Decisions
└── Relationships
```

The Knowledge Graph does not replace the original Documents.

Documents remain the authoritative sources of evidence.

The Knowledge Graph organizes the meaning extracted from those sources.

Every Knowledge Object and Relationship must preserve traceability to its origin whenever possible.

Conceptually:

```text
Source Document
      │
      ▼
Extracted Information
      │
      ▼
Knowledge Object
      │
      ▼
Business Relationship
      │
      ▼
Knowledge Graph
```

The Knowledge Graph enables ENQI to:

- Connect information across multiple Documents
- Identify dependencies between business entities
- Discover patterns that are not visible in isolated sources
- Preserve organizational context
- Support explainable AI responses
- Improve search and discovery
- Detect risks and inconsistencies
- Support strategic and operational reasoning

The graph continuously evolves as new information enters the platform.

Existing Knowledge Objects may receive:

- New attributes
- New sources
- New relationships
- Updated confidence
- Human validation
- Corrections
- Historical states

A Knowledge Graph is always scoped to an authorized organizational context.

Knowledge from different Organizations must never be combined.

Knowledge from different Workspaces may only be connected through explicit, authorized and auditable mechanisms.

# Knowledge Objects

A Knowledge Object represents a business concept understood by ENQI.

Examples include:

- Customer
- Supplier
- Contract
- Invoice
- Product
- Service
- Project
- Asset
- Risk
- Obligation
- Policy
- Procedure
- Decision
- Business Event

A Knowledge Object may be generated from one or multiple sources.

It may contain:

- Unique identity
- Business type
- Attributes
- Organizational context
- Workspace
- Source references
- Confidence level
- Validation status
- Creation date
- Last update date
- Relationships
- Historical changes

Knowledge Objects must not duplicate the original Documents.

They represent the business meaning derived from those Documents.

Example:

```text
Document
    Contract-2026-014.pdf

Extracted Information
    Supplier: Atlas Ltd.
    Expiration Date: 2027-03-31
    Contract Value: R$ 850,000
    Renewal Clause: Automatic

Knowledge Objects
    Supplier
    Contract
    Financial Commitment
    Renewal Obligation
```

Knowledge Objects form the nodes of the ENQI Knowledge Graph.

Relationships form the connections between those nodes.

Together, they create a reusable representation of organizational understanding.

# Knowledge Traceability

Every Knowledge Object and Relationship should remain traceable to the information that supports it.

Traceability may include:

- Source Document
- Document Version
- Page
- Section
- Extracted passage
- Workflow
- Human input
- AI analysis
- Creation timestamp
- Validation history

Conceptually:

```text
Knowledge Object
      │
      ├── Source Document
      ├── Document Version
      ├── Extracted Evidence
      ├── AI Analysis
      └── Human Validation
```

Traceability enables:

- Explainability
- Auditing
- Error correction
- Compliance review
- Confidence evaluation
- Evidence-based AI responses

ENQI should never present inferred Knowledge as verified fact without preserving its confidence and origin.

Organizational trust depends on the ability to explain where Knowledge came from.

---

# Knowledge Validation

Not every piece of organizational Knowledge has the same level of certainty.

ENQI explicitly distinguishes between inferred, validated and verified Knowledge.

Every Knowledge Object may contain a validation status.

Possible validation states include:

- Extracted
- Inferred
- AI Validated
- Human Reviewed
- Approved
- Deprecated
- Archived

Conceptually:

```text
Document
      │
      ▼
Information Extraction
      │
      ▼
Knowledge Object
      │
      ▼
AI Validation
      │
      ▼
Human Review
      │
      ▼
Approved Knowledge
```

Knowledge confidence continuously evolves.

Confidence may increase when:

- Additional supporting documents are found
- Human experts validate the information
- Independent sources confirm the same fact
- Business workflows reinforce the relationship

Confidence may decrease when:

- Contradictory information appears
- Documents expire
- Regulations change
- Human reviewers reject the inference
- Organizational context changes

Every validation event should be auditable.

Validation history must never be lost.

AI may propose Knowledge.

Humans approve organizational truth.

Organizational trust depends on transparent validation.

# Knowledge Evolution

Organizational Knowledge is never static.

Organizations continuously evolve.

People change.

Processes change.

Products change.

Customers change.

Regulations change.

Markets change.

Knowledge must evolve together with the organization.

Knowledge Objects are therefore living business representations.

They may evolve by:

- Receiving new attributes
- Receiving new relationships
- Receiving additional evidence
- Increasing confidence
- Decreasing confidence
- Being corrected
- Being merged
- Being split
- Becoming obsolete
- Being archived

Conceptually:

```text
Document
      │
      ▼
Knowledge Object v1
      │
      ▼
New Documents
      │
      ▼
Knowledge Update
      │
      ▼
Knowledge Object v2
      │
      ▼
Business Decision
      │
      ▼
New Organizational Context
      │
      ▼
Knowledge Object v3
```

Knowledge should preserve its historical evolution.

Organizations must always be able to answer:

- What did we know?
- When did we know it?
- Why did it change?
- Which evidence supported the change?
- Who approved the update?
- Which business decision was affected?

Knowledge evolution is essential for:

- Organizational learning
- Continuous improvement
- Regulatory compliance
- Explainable AI
- Risk management
- Historical auditing

ENQI should never overwrite organizational understanding.

It should preserve the evolution of that understanding over time.

Knowledge is not a snapshot.

It is a continuously evolving representation of organizational reality.
